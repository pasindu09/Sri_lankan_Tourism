Central Highlands Of Sri Lanka

The highlands of Sri Lanka are found in the island's south-central region. The Peak Wilderness Protected Area, Horton Plains National Park, and Knuckles Conservation Forest are all part of the property. These montane forests, which climb to 2,500 meters above sea level, are home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, including endangered species like the western-purple-faced langur, Horton Plains slender loris, and Sri Lankan leopard. The area is regarded as a biodiversity hotspot.

Visit Bakers Fall In Hortain Plaines

Baker's falls is a spectacular and stunning waterfall located in the Nuwara Eliya area, 3 kilometers from the main gate of Horton Plains national park. The climb to the falls, named for late Sir Samuel Baker, a well-known British explorer and game hunter during the British colonial period, provides breathtaking views over Horton Plains. 'Baker's Loop,' which is part of the circular trail that runs around Horton Plains from its entrance to World's End, is where the falls may be seen. It is also accessible from the towns of Pattipola and Ohiya. A recognized pathway leads from Pattipola to World's End and loops back to Baker's Falls.

Sacred City of Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura, Ceylon's governmental and religious capital, was abandoned during an invasion in 993. In the 3rd century B.C., Sanghamitta, the founder of a Buddhist nuns' order, brought a cutting from the Buddha's fig tree, the 'tree of enlightenment, to this sacred city. The sacred city of Anuradhapura is one of Sri Lanka's eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites and the country's first ancient capital. The city of Anuradhapura is home to a large network of historic temple shrines and monasteries that span 40 square kilometers. It is the city with the greatest concentration of cultural landmarks. Thousands of years later, the majority of these Buddhist temples have been beautifully kept. Buddhist pilgrims hold it in high regard as one of Sri Lanka's most sacred cities.

Where To Be Located

Anuradhapura is Sri Lanka's first capital, located in the north central province. It is one of Sri Lanka's ancient capitals, and for many centuries, it was the epicenter of Theravada Buddhism. The Sacred City of Anuradhapura was designated as a UNESCO world heritage site in 1982 due to the remnants of an ancient Sri Lankan civilization.

What To Be Seen

Anuradhapura is home to a number of noteworthy tourist attractions.
